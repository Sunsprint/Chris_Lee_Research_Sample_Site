Compliant Structures

Tessellating Pseudo-origami Landing Gear & Crash Equipment

A picture of the accelerometer test equipment used in gathering kinematic data

Using compliant structures made of simple paper and tape, we tested their applicability in drone avionics and crash testing. The prototypes tested benefit from their cheap cost of manufacture, lightweightedness, and passive behaviors with no requirement of active electronics or control mechanisms.

This research has been accepted into the 2024 American Society of Mechanical Engineer's (ASME) International Design Engineering Technical & Computers and Information in Engineering Conference (IDETC-CIE 2024), where Professor Chris Lee and the research students involved will present in Washington D.C. in August 2024.

Final Car Testing Rig

Custom 3D-printed and lasercut mounting shells strapped to a commercial RC toy car and outfitted with sensors, an Arduino, and battery pack

Initial Car Testing Rig

3D-printed smartphone mount (pictured with prototype attached)

A remote-controlled toy car was cannibalized for data collection purposes- initially the accelerometer attached was a simple smartphone. This proved somewhat unreliable and yielded unwarranted spikes in data collection, so an accelerometer and IR distance sensor hooked up to an Arduino unit fell into favor.

Data samples were collected by simply ramming the RC car at full speed into a wooden panel weighed down by barbell weights while the given prototyp was attached.
